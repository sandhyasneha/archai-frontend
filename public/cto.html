The pitch

Cloud architecture, at software speed and software cost.

Enterprise cloud architecture today means $500/hour consulting engagements, weeks of back-and-forth, and Terraform written by hand — with security and compliance reviewed after the fact, if at all. ArchAI replaces that with a validated, multi-agent AI pipeline: describe the system in plain English, or point it at what you already have, and get production-ready, audited Terraform in minutes — for AWS, Azure, and GCP, starting free.

The moat

Why this is defensible, not just a wrapper on an LLM.

01

Validated output, not raw generation

A dedicated Auditor agent checks every blueprint for syntax and security issues before you ever see it — retrying automatically on failure. Most AI coding tools hand you unvalidated output and call it done.

02

Compounding switching cost via the Knowledge Base

Upload your naming conventions, approved instance types, and compliance requirements once — every future blueprint enforces them automatically. The more your team uses it, the more expensive it becomes to leave.

03

Full lifecycle, not a point solution

Design, security audit, multi-cloud cost comparison, disaster recovery planning, and brownfield migration — in one platform. Most competitors solve one slice (execution, governance, or codification) and expect you to stitch the rest together.

04

SMB-priced, enterprise-capable

Comparable governance-and-generation platforms price for platform teams at $349–$1,500+/month with custom quotes above that. ArchAI starts free and reaches full capability at $199/month — accessible from the first hire, not just at scale.
